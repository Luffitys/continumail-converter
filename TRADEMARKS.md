The GPL gives you broad rights over the **code**: you may use, study, modify,
redistribute, and sell it, including building and distributing your own version.

When you distribute a modified version, you must:

- **Remove or replace the ContinuMail name, logos, and brand assets.** Your fork

Nominative reference is fine — e.g. stating truthfully that your project "is based

Converter." Using the marks as the identity of your own product is not.

## Why

ContinuMail follows an open-core model: this converter is free and open-source
forever, while the ContinuMail **brand** distinguishes the official, supported
builds and the separate paid products. Keeping the trademark separate from the
code license lets the code stay maximally free while protecting users from
confusingly branded forks.
